# Introduction

This is my first project that explores the data job market with a focus on Data Analyst roles.
It examines top-paying jobs, in-demand skills, and the intersection of high
demand and high salary to identify the most optimal skills to learn.

# Background
This project was built alongside SQL practice as a mostly independent work.
Using a sample job postings database, the goal was to answer the following
questions through structured queries:

1. What are the top-paying jobs for my role?
2. What are the skills required for the top-paying roles?
3. What are the most in-demand skills for my role?
4. What are the top skills based on salary for my role?
5. What are the most optimal skills to learn?

# Tools I Used

- **SQL** for querying and analyzing the job postings database
- **PostgreSQL** as the database management system
- **VS Code** for writing and executing queries
- **Git and GitHub** for version control and sharing

# The Analysis

Each query targeted a specific question:

### 1. Top-paying Data Analyst jobs

The goal was to identify the highest-paying Data Analyst roles,
with a specific focus on remote positions. The query below surfaces
these opportunities:

```sql
SELECT
    job_id,
    job_title,
    job_location,
    job_schedule_type,
    salary_year_avg,
    job_posted_date,
    name AS company_name
FROM
    job_postings_fact
LEFT JOIN company_dim ON job_postings_fact.company_id = company_dim.company_id
WHERE
    job_title_short = 'Data Analyst' AND 
    job_location = 'Anywhere' AND
    salary_year_avg IS NOT NULL
ORDER BY salary_year_avg DESC
LIMIT 10
```
**Analysis**
- **The salary range is wide:** from $184,000 at the lower end to $650,000 at
  the top, with the Mantys outlier nearly doubling the next highest offer
  from Meta at $336,500. This suggests significant variance in compensation
  depending on seniority and company.

- **Senior and director-level titles dominate the top spots**. Roles such as
  Director of Analytics, Associate Director, and Principal Data Analyst
  consistently appear above $185,000, indicating that title and scope of
  responsibility are strong salary drivers.

- **Big-name employers like Meta, AT&T, and Pinterest are present**, but
  smaller or specialized firms such as SmartAsset and Motional also offer
  competitive packages above $185,000, showing that high pay is not
  exclusive to large tech companies.

### 2. Skills tied to top-paying roles
The main goal was to identify what are the skills required for the top-paying roles. The query joins the top 10 highest-paying remote Data Analyst roles with their required skills. It uses a CTE to first isolate the top-paying jobs, then expands each job into one row per skill via two joins against the skills tables.

```sql
WITH top_paying_jobs AS (
    SELECT
        job_id,
        job_title,
        salary_year_avg,
        name AS company_name
    FROM
        job_postings_fact
    LEFT JOIN company_dim ON job_postings_fact.company_id = company_dim.company_id
    WHERE
        job_title_short = 'Data Analyst' AND 
        job_location = 'Anywhere' AND
        salary_year_avg IS NOT NULL
    ORDER BY salary_year_avg DESC
    LIMIT 10
)

SELECT
    top_paying_jobs.*,
    skills
FROM top_paying_jobs
INNER JOIN skills_job_dim ON top_paying_jobs.job_id = skills_job_dim.job_id
INNER JOIN skills_dim ON skills_job_dim.skill_id = skills_dim.skill_id
ORDER BY salary_year_avg DESC
```

**Analysis**

- SQL appears in all 8 listed jobs, making it the single most consistent requirement across top-paying roles. Python follows closely, present in 7 out of 8. These two form the non-negotiable foundation.
- Tableau is required by 6 of the 8 roles, making it the dominant visualization tool at this salary level. Power BI appears in 2 roles but always alongside Tableau, never as a standalone alternative.
- Cloud and big data tools (AWS, Azure, Snowflake, Databricks) cluster at the higher end of the salary range, specifically at AT&T ($255k) and Inclusively ($189k), suggesting that cloud proficiency is a salary multiplier rather than a baseline expectation.

### 3. In-Demand skills for Data Analysts
The query counts how many job postings require each skill, filtered for Data Analyst roles. The result ranks skills by raw demand, giving a direct signal of what the market expects most frequently.


```sql
SELECT
    skills,
    COUNT(skills_job_dim.job_id) AS demand_count
FROM job_postings_fact
INNER JOIN skills_job_dim ON job_postings_fact.job_id = skills_job_dim.job_id
INNER JOIN skills_dim ON skills_job_dim.skill_id = skills_dim.skill_id
WHERE job_title_short = 'Data Analyst' AND job_work_from_home = TRUE
GROUP BY skills
ORDER BY demand_count DESC
LIMIT 5;
```

**Analysis**

- SQL leads with 7,291 mentions, nearly 58% more than the second-ranked Excel (4,611). It is not optional at any level of the Data Analyst market.
- Python (4,330) and Tableau (3,745) rank third and fourth, confirming that programming and visualization are core expectations alongside spreadsheet proficiency.
- Power BI (2,609) rounds out the top five. Its presence alongside Tableau suggests employers often list both, rather than treating them as substitutes.

### 4. Skills based on salary
The query calculates the average yearly salary per skill, filtered for remote Data Analyst roles with a known salary. Results are ranked by average salary descending, limited to the top 25.


```sql
SELECT
    skills,
    ROUND(AVG(salary_year_avg), 0) AS avg_salary
FROM job_postings_fact
INNER JOIN skills_job_dim ON job_postings_fact.job_id = skills_job_dim.job_id
INNER JOIN skills_dim ON skills_job_dim.skill_id = skills_dim.skill_id
WHERE 
    job_title_short = 'Data Analyst' 
    AND salary_year_avg IS NOT NULL
    AND job_work_from_home = TRUE
GROUP BY skills
ORDER BY avg_salary DESC
LIMIT 25;
```
**Analysis**

- Big data and distributed computing tools dominate the top: PySpark ($208k), Couchbase ($160k), and Databricks ($141k) all signal that processing large-scale data commands a significant premium.
- DevOps and version control tools such as Bitbucket ($189k), GitLab ($154k), and Kubernetes ($132k) rank surprisingly high, suggesting that analysts who can operate closer to engineering workflows earn more.
- Core data science libraries like Pandas ($151k), NumPy ($143k), and Scikit-learn ($125k) confirm that Python proficiency beyond basic scripting translates directly into higher pay.

### 5. Most optimal skills to learn
The query combines demand count and average salary per skill, filtered for remote Data Analyst roles. A demand threshold of more than 10 postings filters out niche or low-frequency skills, leaving only skills that are both realistically hireable and well-compensated.


```sql
WITH skills_demand AS (
    SELECT
        skills_dim.skill_id,
        skills_dim.skills,
        COUNT(skills_job_dim.job_id) AS demand_count
    FROM job_postings_fact
    INNER JOIN skills_job_dim ON job_postings_fact.job_id = skills_job_dim.job_id
    INNER JOIN skills_dim ON skills_job_dim.skill_id = skills_dim.skill_id
    WHERE
        job_title_short = 'Data Analyst' 
        AND salary_year_avg IS NOT NULL
        AND job_work_from_home = TRUE
    GROUP BY 
        skills_dim.skill_id
), avg_salary AS (
    SELECT
        skills_job_dim.skill_id,
        ROUND(AVG(salary_year_avg), 0) AS avg_salary
    FROM job_postings_fact
    INNER JOIN skills_job_dim ON job_postings_fact.job_id = skills_job_dim.job_id
    INNER JOIN skills_dim ON skills_job_dim.skill_id = skills_dim.skill_id
    WHERE 
        job_title_short = 'Data Analyst' 
        AND salary_year_avg IS NOT NULL
        AND job_work_from_home = TRUE
    GROUP BY skills_job_dim.skill_id
)

SELECT
    skills_demand.skill_id,
    skills_demand.skills,
    demand_count,
    avg_salary
FROM skills_demand
INNER JOIN avg_salary ON skills_demand.skill_id = avg_salary.skill_id
WHERE
    demand_count > 10
ORDER BY
    avg_salary DESC,
    demand_count DESC
LIMIT 25;
```
**Analysis**

- Go ($115k, 27 postings) and Hadoop ($113k, 22 postings) lead on salary among mid-demand skills, but their relatively low counts suggest they are differentiators rather than baseline requirements.
- Snowflake (37 postings, $112k), Azure (34 postings, $111k), and AWS (32 postings, $108k) offer the best balance of demand and salary among cloud tools, making them strong candidates for skill investment.
- Python (236 postings, $101k) and Tableau (230 postings, $99k) have by far the highest demand in the set. Their salaries are lower than niche tools but their volume makes them the safest foundation to build on first.

# What I Learned

Throughout this project I strengthened my SQL skills and applied them to
real-world data questions. The key technical takeaways:

- **Complex queries with CTEs:** Learned to structure multi-step analysis
  using Common Table Expressions, breaking down problems into readable,
  reusable query blocks instead of deeply nested subqueries.

- **Aggregations and joins:** Practiced combining multiple tables with
  INNER and LEFT JOINs, and applied GROUP BY with aggregate functions
  (COUNT, AVG, ROUND) to summarize large datasets into actionable metrics.

- **Filtering and ranking:** Used WHERE clauses to isolate specific roles,
  salary ranges, and work arrangements, combined with ORDER BY and LIMIT
  to surface the most relevant results.

- **Translating business questions into queries:** Each of the five
  questions required thinking through what data was needed, which tables
  held it, and how to connect them. This reinforced the habit of starting
  from the question, not the data.

- **Interpreting results analytically:** Writing the query was only half
  the work. Summarizing and contextualizing the output, such as spotting
  the salary outlier at Mantys or recognizing that cloud skills cluster
  at higher pay bands, built the habit of reading data critically rather
  than just retrieving it.

# Conclusions

## Insights
Drawing from all five analyses, several patterns emerge across the
Data Analyst job market for remote roles:

1. **SQL is non-negotiable.** It leads both demand (7,291 postings) and
   appears in every single top-paying role. No other skill comes close
   to its consistency across salary levels and job types.

2. **Python and Tableau form the second tier.** Python ranks third in
   demand (4,330) and appears in 7 of the top 10 highest-paying roles.
   Tableau follows closely in both demand (3,745) and top-role presence
   (6 of 10). Together with SQL, these three skills cover the foundation
   of most remote Data Analyst positions.

3. **Cloud skills are a salary multiplier.** AWS, Azure, Snowflake, and
   Databricks do not dominate raw demand counts, but they consistently
   appear in roles paying above $180,000. Analysts who add cloud
   proficiency to a SQL and Python foundation position themselves at
   the higher end of the pay range.

4. **Big data tools command the highest average salaries.** PySpark
   ($208k), Hadoop ($113k), and Spark ($99k) rank at or near the top
   of salary-by-skill analysis, even with moderate demand counts. They
   represent a high-reward specialization path.

5. **The most optimal skills balance both dimensions.** Snowflake,
   Azure, and AWS each combine demand counts above 30 with average
   salaries above $108,000, making them the clearest targets for
   skill investment after the SQL, Python, and Tableau baseline
   is established.
